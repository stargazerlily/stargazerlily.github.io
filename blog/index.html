Amelie LeTierce

at Native Plant Trust

A New Opportunity

12/13/2020

Just to start off, my name is Amelie LeTierce, I am 23 years old, and I have been a student of sustainable food and farming in the online program offered by UMass Stockbridge for a little over a year. I came to this program because of my history working in America's front-end of produce and floriculture: the supermarket, and because of my growing interest in sustainable lifestyles, particularly growing my own veggies. I also find that the flexible nature of asynchronous offered usually works well for me. Since enrolling I have grown tremendously as a gardener and young botanist, and I couldn't be more grateful for the range of experiences and inspiration I have gained, but as I grow I find there are many elements of plant science which elude my online-only, ag-centric education.

I'm pretty dorky about loving my veggies, and I'm hoping to score some MA FOOD plates soon, now that I have a car registered in Massachusetts. The extra registration charge goes to supporting young farmers like me, and it will help my car stand out now that I will be missing my lucky RI plates...

I want to gain hands-on experience of working with plants in both research and field settings. I would love to study natural plant ecology and systematics and become familiar with the diversity of plant life on our Earth and particularly in my home region of New England. Ideally I would like my education to ultimately leave me with the flexibility to pursue employment in many areas of plant biology. With my experience I could be a farmer, a teacher, a florist, or a scientist, whichever comes most naturally with where I am at and where I am going. While researching potential internships, I specifically looked for opportunities to broaden these experiences.

When I first reached out to Native Plant Trust, I was not expecting they would have any immediate educational opportunities for me. I was interested in learning more about their summer internships, and I thought maybe I could participate as a volunteer at the tail-end of their fieldwork season. It turned out I was a little late to go on any outings, but there were a few off-season projects I was welcome to be involved in!

For about two months now I have been working with the plant conservation department at Garden in the Woods in Framingham, MA. Before meeting the wonderful folks at NPT, I had worked in grocery and retail for 5 years, making this my first semi-professional experience in the field of plant sciences. In many ways I am as anxious as I am delighted about the prospect.

"Conserving and promoting New England’s native plants to ensure healthy, biologically diverse landscapes"

Native Plant Trust (formerly New England Wildflower Society) is a non-profit organization dedicated to monitoring and maintaining the plant diversity of New England. They promote the value of native plant species through education on the topics of botany, ecology, and conservation, and operate a native plant nursery for the sales of native landscape plants, a botanical garden to visit and explore, and several natural conservatories around New England.
